Slide 02 — Problem

Three household
crises. One product gap.

Financial dysfunction in families is not an information problem. It is a visibility, coordination, and behaviour problem — and no product today addresses all three simultaneously for the household as a unit.

Crisis 01
Invisible Money
The average household loses £340/month to spending they cannot see. 3 in 5 adults never track spending. 61% cannot cover a £1,000 emergency without debt. By the time awareness arrives, the cash is gone.
Crisis 02
Partner Misalignment
89% of divorcing couples cite financial disagreements as a primary driver. Both partners operating in separate financial mental models — no shared view, no shared language, no shared plan. The "money conversation" is universally dreaded.
Crisis 03
Generational Trap
Children in households with no financial education inherit the same broken habits. £78T intergenerational wealth transfer is approaching. Families without financial literacy will miss it entirely. No product today breaks this cycle at the family unit level.
