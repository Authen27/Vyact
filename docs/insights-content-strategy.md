# Vyact — Content Strategy (One Page)

> **Strategist's thesis:** Vyact is not becoming a media company. Stickiness comes from reflecting the user's *own money* back as bite-sized content, seasoned with a small amount of curated free knowledge. Zero content treadmill, never stale, impossible to commoditize.

## The core principle
The stickiest content in a finance app isn't articles about money — it's **the user's money shown back to them as content**. A generic "5 ways to save" competes with all of Google and is obsolete on publish. *"You spent ₹8,400 on dining in June — 34% above your usual"* is unique, fresh on every transaction, and free to produce. Build the engine once; it runs forever.

## Three streams (and their ratio)

| Stream | What | Share | Cost to run |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Personal insights** | The user's own data as swipeable cards (mirrors, streaks, anomalies, forecasts, Pulse moments) | **~60%** | **Zero** ongoing — templates over the existing aggregation engine |
| **2. Evergreen library** | A fixed set of 100+ visual+text micro-articles on timeless principles, surfaced *contextually* | **~25%** | **One-time** authoring; then a static asset |
| **3. Curated external** | Hand-picked items from *free* authoritative sources (RBI, SEBI, govt), made personal by pairing with the user's data | **~15%** | **~30 min/week** of curation (select + tag, never write) |

## Tone (non-negotiable)
**Neutral-to-positive, always.** Finance is emotionally loaded; a feed that keeps saying "you overspent" gets muted. Lead with neutral reflection and positive reinforcement; earn the right to flag a problem, and when you do, frame it as a next step, never a verdict. This lets users absorb information **gradually instead of in shocks**.

## The anti-doomscroll contract
Users live in feed-world; meet them there, but invert the dark pattern. **One insight per card, 5-second read, big number + one line.** A "Today" surface of 3–5 fresh cards that *deliberately ends* — finitude is the trust signal. Tap any card to expand into the relevant page or a Planner/Interpret answer. The card is the hook; the app is the depth.

## What we will NOT do
News scraping for engagement (legal + quality risk) · generic "top-10 hacks" listicles (commoditized, trust-eroding) · market predictions / stock tips (India regulatory minefield, off-brand) · LLM-generated *numbers or advice* (violates "services compute, never fabricate").

## Management model (zero content team)
- **Stream 1** self-runs — templates + the data layer. Add a template occasionally; otherwise hands-off.
- **Stream 2** = a one-time authoring of 100+ cards, uploadable via admin or direct-to-DB. A fixed library, refreshed only when a principle genuinely changes.
- **Stream 3** = a lightweight admin config surface: paste/curate a free-source item, attach a one-line "why this matters," set trigger tags. ~30 min/week, not a publishing calendar.
- **Ranking** is rules-based (same philosophy as the Planner): every card carries trigger conditions; the engine picks who sees what. No editorial calendar, no daily pressure.

## Free, authoritative source shortlist (Stream 3)
RBI (press releases, circulars, rate decisions) · SEBI investor-education · Income-Tax Dept (slab/rule changes) · PFRDA/NPS & PPF rate notifications · government scheme bulletins. All public-domain or freely citable; all India-relevant; sorted reverse-chronological in admin. Vyact *aggregates and contextualizes* — it never republishes wholesale.

## Operating cost, summarized
**Build the insight engine once + write 100+ evergreen cards once + ~30 min/week curation.** The app never goes stale because Stream 1 is as fresh as the user's last transaction.
